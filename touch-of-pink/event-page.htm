TOUCH OF PINK (2004) is director Ian Iqbal Rashid’s delightful debut feature. This romantic comedy tells the coming-out story of Alim (Jimi Mistry), a gay, Muslim, Indo-Canadian man who lives with his British boyfriend Giles (Kristen Holden-Ried) in London, UK. When Alim’s watchful and widowed Ismaili mother Nuru (Suleka Mathew) decides to visit from Toronto, he conceals his queerness and seeks emotional guidance from imaginary friend and confidante, the ghost of queer, classic Hollywood moviestar Cary Grant (Kyle McLachlan). A Doris Day-fangirl herself, an unaware Nuru dreams up nuptial prospects for her son. A dramedy of errors ensues, with a heart-warming mother-son relationship at its center. Nodding to the Grant/Day-starrer That Touch of Mink (1962), Rashid’s British-Canadian aughts romp offers an inventive, intergenerational update to the singular cinematic tradition of conservative yet cheeky sex-comedies of 1950s-60s Hollywood. In turns both raucous and tender, TOUCH OF PINK (2004) unfolds as a charmingly risqué, rosily transcontinental, queerly familial affair.
